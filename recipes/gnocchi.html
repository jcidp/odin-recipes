Gnocchi with mushrooms

Description

This creamy sausage gnocchi is a comforting, indulgent dish with ingredients like wine, cream, and shallots. I do not recommend adding salt because I find the sausage has enough salt. Although cheese is optional, I find it gets lost in the sauce.

Ingredients

  • 3 Italian-style turkey sausages, casings removed
  • 4 ounces portobello mushrooms, coarsely chopped
  • 2 shallots, minced
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ cup dry white wine (such as Pinot Grigio)
  • 1 (18 ounce) package potato gnocchi
  • 1 ½ cups heavy cream
  • ½ teaspoon Italian herbs (such as cantanzaro herbs from Savory Spice Shop®)
  • 1 pinch red pepper flakes, or to taste (Optional)
  • salt and ground black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Heat a heavy skillet over medium-high heat
  2. Cook and stir sausage in the hot skillet until crumbly and no longer pink, about 7 minutes.
  3. Add mushrooms, shallots, butter, and oil; cook, stirring occasionally, until mushrooms and shallots are soft and beginning to brown, about 5 minutes
  4. Add garlic and cook for 1 to 2 minutes
  5. Pour in white wine and simmer until wine has reduced by half, about 5 minutes
  6. Meanwhile, bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook gnocchi in boiling water until pasta floats to the top, 2 to 4 minutes. Drain.
  7. Add gnocchi, cream, Italian herbs, pepper flakes, salt, and pepper. Cook over medium-low heat until sauce has thickened, about 10 minutes.
